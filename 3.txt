EPISODE 3: "THE ANTHILL"
TEASER: The Intelligence (Cole’s Nightmare)
INT. UN SECURE FACILITY - COLE'S OFFICE - DAY (CONTINUOUS FROM EP 2)
The room is quiet. Director Marcus Cole stands before his wall-to-wall array of monitors displaying Earth’s real-time seismic readouts.
The Visualization: The monitors render a 3D hologram of the globe, threaded with hundreds of bright red, perfectly straight transit lanes—the ancient orbital paths of the Leviathans moving smoothly through the mantle. He’s watched this predictable traffic for months.
The Reaction: The second Maya’s radar turned on in Munich (Ep 2), a tiny blue blip pulses on the globe.
Instantly, the hundreds of massive red transit lines threading through Earth shimmer. They smoothly, simultaneously bow outward in massive arcs, steering a five-mile berth entirely around the blue blip in Munich.
The horror hits Cole: They swerved.
The entities didn't bump blindly into a new obstacle. The entire herd altered its course seamlessly across the globe in less than a second because they recognized humanity just turned on a light.
Cole (staring at the screens): "They know."
(TITLE CARD: 85%)
ACT I: The Collateral Damage
The Catastrophe (The New Lanes): By shifting their ancient, established paths, the entities inadvertently route their massive gravitational wakes directly beneath major human population centers they previously avoided.
Secondary effect: the abrupt lane change is stirring up ancient shells of dark particulate trapped in near-Earth orbital corridors, a new layer of contamination Cole's satellite teams can barely separate from the larger crisis.
EXT. SHANGHAI, JAPAN - BULLET TRAIN LINE - DAY
The Setup: A high-speed rail line elevated 100 feet in the air above the sprawling city. It is raining heavily.
The Event (The Sound of Gravity): The deep, bone-rattling sub-bass hum (the show’s established auditory cue for Dark Matter proximity) begins.
The falling rain physically arcs sideways into the wind, pulled by an impossible 3G localized lateral gravity sheer.
The reinforced steel pillars supporting the elevated train track don't snap from an impact; they silently groan and buckle outward at a perfect 45-degree angle.
The incoming bullet train derails instantly into the streets below. Tens of thousands are dead or trapped as city blocks structurally warp in the invisible wake. The casualties of Maya’s flashlight begin mounting in real-time.
ACT II: The Fracture (Science vs. Tactics)
INT. MUNICH QUARANTINE LAB - LATER
Dr. Maya Lin and Dr. Elias Vance watch the devastation on a live feed. Maya is hollowed out, drowning in sudden, crushing guilt. The blood on those tracks is hers.
Director Cole enters the quarantine. He lays out the strategy: Deterrence.
Cole's Logic: "Dr. Lin, they swerved because you lit a match. That proves they respond to your X17 field. They didn’t attack; they avoided it. If we build a physical X17 'speedbump' across the Pacific transit lane—if we prove to them we can make their highway solid—they will learn to route entirely around our coastal infrastructure. We don't need a war. We just need to build a fence. Once a route has a cost, behavior changes."
The Betrayal of Math: Elias refuses furiously. He doesn't cite fear; he cites incomplete data.
Elias: "Cole, you’re introducing a massive, unpredictable kinetic variable into a complex gravitational ecosystem based on 72 hours of panicked observation. You have no idea what forces govern their physics, or what they think your fence is. You don't test a hive with a baseball bat; you map it!"
Maya looks at the screens of derailed trains and drowning coastal towns. The arrogance she had when discovering X17 is replaced by an urgent, frantic need for atonement. She must fix what she broke.
Maya agrees with Cole. Elias, disgusted by their lack of scientific patience, grabs his hard drives and locks himself in a sterile data room, severing his partnership with them to map the system alone before they ruin it.
ACT III: The Blank Sky (Elias's A-Plot)
INT. ISOLATION DATA-CENTER - LATE NIGHT
Elias is exhausted, compiling every trajectory vector from Cole’s UN seismic trackers.
The Orbital Threading: As he maps the hundreds of flight paths intersecting Earth's core, he realizes they aren’t staying in the Earth’s mantle. The Leviathans plunge into the planet, slingshot around the molten core using extreme gravitational braking, and fire out the other side.
The Breakthrough: He extrapolates their exit vectors. The lines do not spray out randomly into space. They cluster perfectly, tracing a hyper-dense, singular flight corridor leading out of our solar system on an exact 32-degree inclination to the ecliptic plane.
His orbital overlays are filthy with the same dark residue he noticed in Episode 2—three billion years of transit junk hanging in old approach lanes like industrial runoff on an invisible highway.
The pattern bothers him. It does not look like pursuit geometry. It looks damaged. The spacing is too exact, too exhausted, too committed to one collapsing route. Elias doesn't have proof yet, but the line-work feels less like an invasion vector than a failing guidance system clinging to one last solution.
The Chilling Discovery: He pulls down visual data from deep-space optical telescopes. He overlays the coordinates the entire alien fleet is aggressively targeting.
It is empty, black space. Nothing is there. No stars, no gas giants. Elias realizes they are not visiting Earth; they are using Earth purely as a temporary thruster-block to aim themselves toward a terrifyingly invisible mass hiding in the Kuiper Belt.
ACT IV: The Deep Ocean Trap
EXT. NORTH ATLANTIC OCEAN - DRILLING PLATFORM - NIGHT
Cole and Maya oversee a staggering, Manhattan Project-level military engineering operation in the pouring rain. The UN has tethered three active nuclear submarines to the rig simply to provide the horrific voltage required to continuously synthesize X17 Polymer.
The Cost of Physics (The Cold Burn): Maya, wrapped in heavily lead-lined thermal gear, is personally welding sections of the X17 emitter-cable grid. A Navy technician helping her suddenly seizes up and drops, clutching his chest.
Maya looks at his bare arm: the skin isn't burned by heat. It’s chalk-white, the veins necrotic black. The dark-radiation bleeding across the chiral dimensional bridge causes rapid, sub-cellular frostbite (Cold Burns). Human biology is not meant to be this close to the 85%. Maya is sickened, realizing she is radiating her own people just to power the machine.
When she reaches for the emergency cutoff, her gloved hand misfires and drops the tool straight through the grated floor. Her fingers are already losing precision. She covers the failure instantly and keeps working.
Cole: "The casualties here save ten million in the cities. Start lowering the net."
The massive array of humming, glowing, unstable X17 cables begins descending three miles into the pitch-black water.
ACT V: Two Universes Touch (The Perfect Silence)
INT. RIG CONTROL ROOM / EXT. OCEAN TRENCH - CONTINUOUS
Cole (into comms): "The lane is live. Radar indicates a localized density target approaching. Mach 12 and accelerating."
The Sonar Scream: Inside the rig control room, the ocean-floor sensors paint a horrifying picture of a mass the size of a mountain chain plunging perfectly down the dark orbital groove directly beneath the rig.
The Anticipation: Helicopters hover above the rig. Soldiers brace themselves for an apocalyptic splash, or a deafening roar of water being violently displaced.
Maya (watching the strain gauges on the net): "Target impact in three. Two. One."
The Impact: The massive invisible Leviathan slams directly into the miles-wide X17 chiral bridge at Mach 12.
The Terror of the Sub-Bass: The subsonic gravity-hum doesn’t explode. The roar of the helicopter blades vanishes. The crash of the waves against the rig’s pylons ceases.
The entire North Atlantic instantly drops into an impossible, ear-splitting, perfect vacuum of sound.
The wind hangs motionless. Two violently incompatible realities—14 billion years apart—have just collided head-on through an artificial polymer thread. The physics engine of the universe temporarily short-circuits.
On the rig console, the analog pressure needle pegs instantly while the digital strain monitor hangs frozen for one impossible beat before catching up.
In the suffocating, physics-breaking dead silence, Maya watches the primary pressure dial on the X17 bridge snap instantly from 0 to absolute maximum.

Writer’s Notes for Episode 3:
Cole is Correct: By portraying Cole's strategy (The Fence) as a brilliant defensive mechanism, we secure the audience's trust in him. He is logical. When the net fails in Ep 4, it isn’t because the humans were stupid—it’s because the physics was overwhelmingly hostile.
Cole's Blind Spot: He is not wrong to seek structure. He is wrong to assume structure is universally legible. The Fence works as character because it is brilliant statecraft applied to a reality that does not recognize statecraft.
The Sub-Sonic Motif Pays Off: Using the dead-silence as the impact rather than a generic Michael Bay explosion gives the end of the episode profound weight.

Planet Nine Early Signal: The "damaged route" language starts preparing the audience to read Planet Nine as tragic infrastructure failure rather than a late-story invasion fakeout.
